Diagnosis. The number of stridulatory teeth>100 (<92 or less in the nominate subspecies) (compare A and B in Figure 6), the male cerci almost straight (at least apex is inward in the nominate subspecies) (compare A and B in Figure 9), the female subgenital plate with widely rounded posterior margin (almost straight in the nominate subspecies) and male paraproct almost straight at posterior margin (rounded in the nominate subspecies) are main characters diagnosing P. inflatus lyciae from the nominate subspecies. Additionally animals from Demre and Kemer can be distinguished by smaller body size.
